I actually think the bronze spearhead pushes the identity in a stronger direction than the fish poster did. It introduces a material language: **bronze, patina, carved stone, lacquer, silk, and mist**. That feels much more like a nation than simply "blue water."

I would narrow the palette even further. One of the strongest mistakes national identity systems make is using too many colours. Japan, Switzerland, Apple, Muji, and Braun all succeed partly because their palettes are extremely disciplined.

# Accent philosophy

The accent should **not** be red.

Instead:

* bronze
* jade
* ivory

This immediately distinguishes Goetsu from both modern China and Imperial China.

---

# Materials

Everything should look like it belongs to one of these materials:

* cast bronze
* carved limestone
* lacquered wood
* rice paper
* silk
* porcelain
* calm water
* morning mist

Nothing glossy.

Nothing chrome.

Nothing saturated.

---

# UI philosophy

The palette should be approximately:

* **55%** Rice Paper
* **25%** Deep Lake Blue
* **10%** Stone Greys
* **7%** Bronze
* **3%** Willow Green

Bronze should feel precious. If every button is bronze, it stops feeling ceremonial.

---

## I would also introduce one "national jade"

This is the one thing missing from the current identity.

```
Goetsu Jade
#6F8574
```

Use it extremely sparingly:

* success
* environmental projects
* selected map regions
* subtle highlights

Because jade has deep historical associations with the lower Yangtze and ancient Chinese ritual culture, it complements the bronze-and-water aesthetic without competing with the primary blue.
